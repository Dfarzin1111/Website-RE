The Seller's Edge

Pricing Is a Science. Marketing Is an Art.

Most sellers leave money on the table — not because the market is weak, but because their listing wasn't positioned correctly from day one. The first two weeks on the market are the most critical window. Overpriced listings lose momentum fast and buyers assume something is wrong.

My approach is data-first. I build a detailed comparative market analysis using recent sales and active competition in your neighbourhood. Then I price strategically — not based on what you want to net, but based on what qualified buyers are actually paying for comparable properties.

Combined with professional photography, targeted digital marketing, and strong MLS exposure, this approach consistently delivers strong results — more qualified showings, faster timelines, and better final prices.

Common Questions

Seller FAQ

How is my listing price determined?

I prepare a comparative market analysis using real sales data from your neighbourhood — not automated estimates. The right price creates competition and urgency. Too high and you lose momentum; too low and you leave money behind. Getting this right is the single most important decision in the sale.

How long will it take to sell?

Properly priced homes in Montreal's active neighbourhoods typically sell within 2–4 weeks. In competitive areas like Westmount or Outremont, well-presented listings can receive offers within days. The key variable is pricing — overpriced listings stagnate.

What are my costs as a seller?

As a seller in Quebec, you typically cover: the broker commission (which is split with the buyer's broker), notary fees for mortgage discharge, and any repairs agreed to during negotiation. Capital gains tax may apply if this is not your principal residence. I walk you through the full cost picture before you list.

Should I do renovations before listing?

Not always. Some improvements have strong ROI (fresh paint, fixtures, minor repairs) while others don't recover their cost in the sale price. I do a walkthrough and advise on exactly what's worth doing — so you spend where it counts and skip what won't move the needle.
