Seller Intelligence

Why homes sit
too long on the market.

1
Overpricing at launch

The first two weeks generate the most buyer interest. Overpriced listings lose that window and take price reductions that signal weakness to remaining buyers.

2
Wrong buyer profile targeted

A property's ideal buyer is not always obvious. Reaching the right person requires understanding who they are and where they are looking, not just posting on Centris.

3
Preparation skipped to save time

Small investments in decluttering, cleaning, and strategic presentation consistently yield disproportionate returns. Buyers price what they see, not what is potential.

4
Weak offer negotiation

An accepted offer is not a good offer by default. Conditions, timing, and pricing can all be negotiated. The difference often comes down to whether the broker treats this as a conversation or a transaction.

Notre-Dame-de-Grace

NDG is one of Montreal's most stable and sought-after residential neighbourhoods. Tree-lined streets, strong community character, and consistent buyer demand make it a reliably competitive market for well-prepared sellers. The borough attracts families, professionals, and income property investors in equal measure. Semi-detached homes, duplexes, and triplexes account for a large portion of transactions, and multiple-offer situations remain common for properties that are properly positioned.

Key streets include Sherbrooke West, Monkland Avenue, and the upper residential streets approaching Westmount. Proximity to Vendome metro, major hospitals, and downtown Montreal continues to drive demand year over year.

Montreal's most densely populated borough, Cote-des-Neiges offers direct proximity to Mount Royal, the Universite de Montreal, and the city's major hospital network. Ville-Marie

Downtown Montreal's core borough is the city's highest-volume condo market and home to its most significant concentration of luxury high-rises, urban lofts, and mixed-use buildings. Selling here requires a different approach than any other area: buyers are more international, more price-sensitive given rising inventory, and more reliant on marketing quality and presentation to make decisions.

In Q1 2026, Ville-Marie recorded 1,627 new listings and 412 sales. Condo active listings rose 10% year-over-year. Plex values, however, rose 15% over the same period, reflecting investor confidence in the income property segment. Days on market for condos currently average 86 days.

FAQ

Answers to the questions
sellers ask most.

How much does it cost to sell my home in Montreal?

In Quebec, broker commission is negotiated directly between you and your broker and is paid from the proceeds of your sale. There is no upfront cost. Commission is typically shared between the listing broker and the buyer's broker. Additional costs include notary fees (generally the buyer's responsibility in Quebec) and a certificate of location if yours is outdated. Exact rates are discussed at your free evaluation meeting.

How long does it take to sell a property in Montreal in 2026?

As of Q1 and April 2026, average days on market vary significantly by property type and neighbourhood. Island-wide, single-family homes average 46 days, condominiums 56 days, and plexes 48 days. Verdun records among the fastest house transactions at 39 days average. Ville-Marie condos currently average 86 days given rising inventory. Well-priced, well-prepared properties consistently outperform these averages.

Do I need to stage my property before listing?

For condos in Ville-Marie and downtown corridors, professional staging is strongly recommended. Buyers in these markets respond directly to presentation, and the return on a well-staged unit is consistently measurable. For single-family homes and plexes in NDG and Cote-des-Neiges, targeted decluttering, cleaning, and minor improvements typically deliver the strongest return relative to cost. Each property is assessed individually before any recommendation is made.

Is it a good time to sell in Montreal in 2025-2026?

The answer depends on your property type and neighbourhood. Single-family home prices rose 11% year-over-year as of April 2026, and Verdun recorded exceptional Q1 performance with volume up 24%. Condo inventory has risen 18% on the island, giving buyers more selection in that segment. The best time to sell is determined by your specific property, its condition, and your financial objectives. A free evaluation will give you a clear picture.

What is the difference between Centris and MLS?

Centris is Quebec's exclusive real estate listing platform used by all OACIQ-licensed brokers. When a property is listed on Centris, it automatically distributes to MLS (the national Multiple Listing Service) and appears on Realtor.ca, giving your listing national and international buyer visibility beyond Quebec. All transactions by OACIQ brokers are conducted through Centris, which provides the most comprehensive data on the Quebec market.

What does a certificate of location cover and do I need one?

A certificate of location is a document prepared by a licensed surveyor that confirms the property's boundaries, identifies any encroachments, and certifies compliance with municipal bylaws. In Quebec, an up-to-date certificate of location (generally less than 10 years old and reflective of the current state of the property) is required for most sales. If renovations, additions, or structures have changed since your last certificate, a new one will be required. Your broker can advise on whether yours is sufficient before listing.
